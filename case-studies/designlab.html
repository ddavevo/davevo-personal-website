04 - Reflection

Contributing to HCI in the era of AI

Thank you UCSD Design Lab for bringing me on! I valued the opportunity to contribute to the growth of creativity support tools (CSTs) and take initiative in various aspects of the research process. I believe our work positions the potential of AI in a positive direction for human-centered design.

And what did I learn?

High-quality research is accessible

Joining an HCI lab was a big shift from my previous work. I went in thinking I would be using advanced tools and techniques to analyze our findings, but it turns out my team and I mostly used Google Sheets.


It just shows that big ideas don’t always need big tools. This realization reaffirms what I've already accomplished, knowing any design problem can be tackled by asking the right questions and using the right metrics.

It’s not just data, it’s a story

From rigorously classifying data, running new types of analyses each week, and presenting noteworthy findings to the PhD and PI, I grew a deeper understanding on how to effectively connect qualitative and quantitative data together that lets our research angle speak for itself.
