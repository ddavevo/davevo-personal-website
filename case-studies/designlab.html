01 - Background

The potential for problem-solving with AI tools

The recent rollout of chat-based AI tools has expedited the problem-solving process for many different fields. LLMs empower people to develop their ideas quickly. But their usability has a ways to go, so it‚Äôs important to understand how the dynamic between humans and AI impacts critical thinking and creative outcomes.

My Role

Introducing The UCSD Design Lab

The UCSD Design Lab conducts research that blends design and social computing. I worked under the guidance of 1 PhD lead to redesign the research prototype‚Äôs interface, draft study protocols, conduct interviews, code and analyze data, and co-author the research paper.

Problem

Catching up to speed with the research project

Our angle was to explore an alternative approach for interacting with LLMs. Instead via chat, we looked at design templates, a well-established strategy for helping people think, organize information, and reflect on creative work.


From an exploratory study conducted earlier in the project timeline, we revisited two major limitations with using LLMs within templates. Here is how we planned to revise the prototype:

‚ùå

Lacks user guidance

Users got stuck on how to write prompts, reflect critically on LLM responses, and extract key insights.

ü§î

→ Use actionable language to guide users toward relevant steps of their thinking process.

‚ùå

Not immediately usable

Users found LLM responses to be wordy, inaccurate, and/or repetitive, wanting more unique insights and clearer ways to leverage them.

ü§î

→ Rework LLM generation features to introduce users to new, concise insights rather than to reflect on existing ones.

Preview

Instead of applying LLM generation to just reflective thinking, we redesigned the plugin prototype to focus on helping users generate insights faster, giving them more user control over which parts of the problem to tackle and which aspects of the solution to build on.


So in our follow-up study, we reframed the research question to analyze how different degrees of LLM integration affects the thinking process and the quality of users‚Äô ideas.
