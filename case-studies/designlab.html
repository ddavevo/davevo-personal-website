01 - Background

The potential for problem-solving with AI tools

The recent rollout of chat-based AI tools have changed problem-solving for many different fields. Being able to gather insights and explore problem spaces just by typing a prompt into an LLMs provides great value, letting users quickly develop their ideas.


But to make the most out of these tools, it‚Äôs important to understand how the dynamic between humans and AI impacts critical thinking and creative outcomes.


The UCSD Design Lab conducts research that blends design and social computing together. I worked in a team of 4 research assistants under the guidance of 1 PhD lead to redesign the research prototype‚Äôs interface, draft study protocols, conduct interviews, code and analyze data, and co-author the research paper.

Problem

Catching up to speed with prior work

Our angle was to explore an alternative approach for interacting with LLMs. Instead via chat, we looked at design templates, a well-established strategy for helping people think, organize information, and reflect on creative work.


From an exploratory story conducted earlier in the project timeline, we revisited two major limitations with LLMs:

Lacks user guidance

Users got stuck on how to write prompts, reflect critically on LLM responses, and extract key insights.

Instead of applying LLM generation to just reflective thinking, we redesigned the plugin prototype to focus on helping users generate insights faster, giving them more user control over which parts of the problem to tackle and which aspects of the solution to build on.


So in our follow-up study, we reframed the research question to analyze how different degrees of LLM integration affects the thinking process and the quality of users‚Äô ideas.
