02 - Background

What our client wants to achieve through a redesign

SNS Salon & Beauty Supply relies heavily on word-of-mouth and repeat customers, but lacked an online presence that reflected the quality of its in-store experience.

In our client briefing with the owner, she wanted the website to attract younger customers and support long-term growth with referrals and online reviews.

03 - Discovery

Auditing the original website

After taking a closer look at their current website, we found that visual clutter and usability issues hindered the website's ability to communicate the salon brand and core specialties.

Problem

The need for visual storytelling

Usability issues make navigation hard

Customer Discovery

Talking with salon-goers to polish our perspective

The client’s perspective on loyalty set an initial direction, but conversations with salon-goers clarified how customers evaluate a salon when deciding to book.

❌

Trust must be shown, not told

Customers expected to see real examples of hair and nail work easily. Several users said they would not book without browsing images, even if they trusted recommends.

❌

Booking was complex and uncertain

The number of dropdowns and unclear service distinctions made users hesitant, especially on mobile. Even loyal customers avoided booking through the site and preferred texting or calling instead.

❌

Users weren’t sold on the salon yet

Although word-of-mouth was on the pulse, customers also relied on online reviews to vet the salon’s quality—suggesting the website wasn’t doing enough to establish credibility.

04 - Design

Prioritizing for customer conversion

Due to limited access to internal data, we prioritized qualitative signals and scoped the work to two critical user journeys:

  • Salon service exploration
  • Booking an appointment

Secondary features (e.g., accounts, embedded reviews) were explored, but deprioritized to focus on clarity and confidence in the core flows.

WIP: Homepage

The hero was redesigned to establish credibility before asking users to book. Past work imagery is shown immediately, while promotions are supported through navigation banners rather than taking up the page.

To encourage exploration over premature booking, primary calls-to-action were redirected toward services and staff profiles—allowing users to understand offerings and expertise before committing.

WIP: Services

The services experience was redesigned to support comparison and faster decision-making. Services were grouped under clear subheaders and displayed as structured web elements rather than images, improving readability, consistency, and mobile usability.

To support a “show, not tell” approach, visual examples and links to the Gallery were introduced alongside service descriptions. Additional CTAs for booking and gift cards, service suggestions, and color-blocked categories helped users navigate options without feeling overwhelmed.

WIP: Booking

The booking flow was simplified by consolidating related services into a smaller set of curated dropdowns. Service cards were standardized with images, pricing, and clear descriptions to make differences easier to understand at a glance.

To encourage exploration without commitment, Vagaro’s Add to Cart and add-on features were introduced, allowing users to adjust selections and reduce clutter from redundant service variants.

07 - Reflection

Informing design guidelines for future AI-assisted creativity tools

Learning

Humanizing our key findings

I thought research was only about pushing the envelope and finding novelty. Over these 10 months, I realized that results only matter if they reflect something about us, the people who use and get influenced by this type of technology.

Challenge

Doing academic research for the first time

It’s no secret that academic research demands rigor and many hats to be worn. While it took time to get up to speed with the literature and methods, the biggest lesson was staying present, being a self-starter, and staying hungry for more.

What I would have done differently

Starting with a storytelling perspective

In hindsight, I see how easy it was to get lost in patterns and numbers without a clear anchor. We had coding schemes, but next time I’d lean less on proving absolutes and more on using data to tell stories about how people think/do.
