03 - Research

Driving salon visits with user-centered design

Primary Research

Learning what motivates people to get salon services

To increase engagement with the redesign, it's crucial to learn what motivates people to visit salons and what information they want to know.


We interviewed 12 salon clients, including 3 from SNS Salon & Beauty Supply, asking about their booking habits and what keeps them coming back, then had them do a think-aloud website walkthrough.


Due to the variety in the responses, it was hard to find immediate trends. So, I proposed we used an affinity diagram to organically categorize our data.

üí°

At first, we thought customer habits would vary by age, but we found patterns in how people form relationships with their salon and what they value most in the experience.

Competitive Analysis

Applying best practices in web design

Now that we have a better understanding of the customer base, we needed to know how to blend user needs and design conventions in order to suit our client's business goals.


And so we ran a competitive analysis on 10+ websites, catering in and outside of the nail tech field to gather a diverse pool of examples to reference from.

From the nail salon websites we looked through, we saw common patterns in the information architecture.

üí°

We notice how few salons highlight AVEDA affiliation or staff expertise. SNS could take advantage of these strengths for a competitive edge.
