01 - Background

College students are booked and busy

Despite burnout and sleep deprivation, students work harder than ever to succeed in a competitive job market after getting their degrees. For UC San Diego students, the demands of a fast-paced quarter system, high coursework loads, job hunting, and life obligations pose significant challenges on learning and personal development.

Looking for a recharge?

UCSD had invested in MetroNaps™ sleeping pods since 2019 for students to use at RIMAC, the most popular campus gym.

The Problem

Although MetroNaps™ sleeping pods have found success in many corporate workspaces and recognizable brands like Google and FedEx, our exploratory field research at RIMAC found that they are not effectively helping students rest because:

❌

Poor sleeping environment

Noisy due to the nearby gym equipment and heavy foot traffic.

❌

Lack of cleanliness

The seats inside were visibly sweaty, odorous, and partially broken.

❌

Limited usage

Most students who sat inside used them briefly, either out of unfamiliarity or due to the environment making it hard to rest.

Solution Overview

We redesigned the sleeping pods to enhance rest quality by making the space easier to maintain and the sleeping experience more pleasant, improving workout recovery for gym-goers and expanding usage to support students overcome burnout & improve academic performance.

02 - Research

Expanding the sleeping pods' audience to students


Secondary Research

After gaining some initial insights about the sleeping pods, I decided to conduct online research to deepen my understanding of self-sufficient public spaces, workout recovery, and academic burnout. Here is what we learned:


Diffusion of responsibility

When people wait for someone else to act instead (in this case, a cleaning staff member), the experience becomes less satisfactory for everyone if nobody steps up.

Aromatherapy

There's an intersection between muscle repair, stress relief, and improved memory & attention through scent. In addition, aroma is one factor that can solve cleanliness.

Primary Research

We conducted 13 interviews with gym-goers, gym staff, and the general student body to learn more about their respective pain points and what could be done better.


The user interviews were either scheduled over Zoom or done on-site at the gym or at popular study spots. After coding the data, here is what we learned:

62%

of participants pointed to a lack of cleanliness via sight, smell, and/or touch.

“I think there should be a better cleaning system for it because usually when I used to use it, I'd kind of get itchy and in my brain, I'm just like, oh, there's people that used it before.”

62%

of participants mentioned that the sleeping pods were located too far away from where they usually are to use regularly.

“I honestly just forget that they're available to students a lot, it’s really far from all the major areas.”

54%

of participants cited privacy and awkwardness concerns with sleeping in a public space.

“I've seen people are a little afraid or awkward to go to the nap pods in RIMAC. I just think it's not really a good thing if people are afraid to sleep or feel awkward to sleep in a public space.”

User Personas

Based on the data we've gathered, we created user personas to distill our findings in order to understand the background, goals, and obstacles of both current gym-goers and the potential general student body.




Opportunities

1. How might we encourage users to take care of the sleeping pods and ensure that existing and potential users enjoy using them?


2. How might the sleeping pods facilitate better rest quality and boost productivity for students after using them?


3. How might we make the sleeping pods more accessible for students across campus?

03 - UI Design

Designing the Interface


User Flow

Since our users are going to be stressed or fatigued when using the interface, we prioritized ease of use when designing the user flow.

Paper Prototyping

To test the user flows, I quickly drafted rough screens from paper. Then, we conducted preliminary user testing by having participants walk through the features. Here’s what we discovered:

❌

Too many steps

Users felt like the process was too complicated and pointed out how annoying it would be as a returning user, having to go through that process each time.

❌

Unclear signifiers

Certain icons and selections were not intuitive, like how some users were confused about how to increase/decrease the timer.

❌

Stressful imagery

The timer counting down should not be so prominent on the resting screen, as the participants associate it with deadlines, exactly what they’re trying to get away from!

06 - Usability Testing

Testing the Aromacovery Experience


Pictured below is the kiosk showcase, where passersby visited and tried out Aromacovery. With a higher fidelity, we conducted usability testing with several eager participants. This is how people reacted:

✅

Easy and personalized

“The screens were simple and really easy to understand and there were a lot of settings so that was nice for customization.”

✅

Immersive

“I like that you actually have a diffuser. It’s so cool that I’m actually smelling the scents and experiencing it for real.”

Future Iterations


Improve ergonomics

“I wish the screen could be tilted towards me, it took a couple tries for the screen to know that I tapped the button.”

Usher more clearly into rest stage

“The counter is counting down, but I want that ‘Okay, now you’re done selecting, go ahead and relax’, a little bit of feedback there.”

Enhance the emotional experience

“For the rest stage, I would also suggest doing something more ambient, like maybe I’m looking at nature in some way, and have it less focused on actions when they should be more subtle.”

07 - Reflection

The Scent of Opportunity


No two places have the same people or the same user needs. There needs to be more consideration for how the sleeping pods are set up and maintained when transported to public settings in order to deliver an accessible, enjoyable sleeping experience.


It's very clear that these sleeping pods have the power to improve the recovery experience beyond the campus gym at UCSD. And so, more usability testing could be done with Aromacovery to expand its use cases to cater to a wider pool of needs, whether at the office, the library, or anywhere someone would need to rejuvenate, restore, and resume their activities.
